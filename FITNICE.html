Project 1: FITNICE

Your Journey to Fitness Starts Here…

In today's fast-paced world, it can be challenging for busy professionals to find time for fitness. FITNICE is a responsive fitness app that was designed to help these individuals manage their workout schedules and stay on track with their fitness goals. The app also helps beginners find the best practices and demonstrate the movements and provides a platform for users to connect with each other and support each other in staying active.

My Process

Empathize

The design process began with extensive research into the needs and challenges faced by busy professionals and beginners in their fitness journeys. This research provided valuable insights into the features and functionality required in the app.

I conducted a user research with a total of 109 participants. 101 participants took part in my survey, which aimed to gain a deeper understanding of my fitness app users and tailor follow-up interview questions. The remaining 8 participants were interviewed, ranging in age from early 30s to early 40s and representing various professional backgrounds. All interviews were conducted virtually and lasted 30 minutes.

I created personas and user stories based on my research findings:

  • As a new user, I want to learn about different exercises, so that I can figure out what is best for me.
  • As a new user, I want to be shown how the exercises are done, so that I know I'm doing them correctly.
  • As a frequent user, I want to be able to schedule exercises for working out, so that I build positive habits.
  • As a frequent user, I want to be able to earn achievements or rewards, so that I can stay motivated.
  • As a frequent user, I want to complete daily challenges, so that I can have an additional way to stay motivated.
  • As a frequent user, I want to track progression and record what I've done, so that I can see my progress over time.
  • As a frequent user, I want to be able to share routines with my friends who may also be interested, so that I can encourage them to become healthier.

Define

  • Overview

In today's fast-paced world, it can be challenging for busy professionals to find time for fitness. FITNICE is a responsive fitness app that was designed to help these individuals manage their workout schedules and stay on track with their fitness goals. The app also helps beginners find the best practices and demonstrate the movements, and provides a platform for users to connect with each other and support each other in staying active.

  • The Problems and Challenges

1. Difficulty in scheduling workout: Busy professionals often struggle to find time for fitness and manage their schedules effectively. The app needed to provide a user-friendly way for users to schedule their workouts and stick to their fitness goals.

2. Difficulty in understanding fitness practices: Beginners often find it difficult to understand the best practices and how to start their fitness journey. The app needed to provide comprehensive information and guidance to help these users get started.

3. Lack of motivation and support: Maintaining a fitness routine can be difficult without the support and motivation of others. The app needed to provide a platform for users to connect with each other and provide encouragement.

  • Solutions

Workout Scheduler: The app features a workout scheduler that allows users to schedule their workouts based on their availability. The scheduler also sends reminders to users to help them stick to their fitness goals. Beginner's Guide: The app provides a comprehensive guide for beginners, including detailed information on best practices, demonstrations of movements, and a step-by-step guide on how to get started. Connect and Support: The app provides a platform for users to connect with each other, share their progress, and provide encouragement and support to each other.

  • The Product

FITNICE is a responsive application that is designed to help you develop a healthier lifestyle by providing interactive guides that include exercises, routines, and tips. You can take your pick from easy and short as 5 minutes routines to more difficult routines that take longer. It's all about starting slowly and steadily building up the amount of time and intensity of your exercise routines.
